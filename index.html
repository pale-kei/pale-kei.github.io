KEI
I don’t think I was meant to exist in the same way as most people.
Not in a dramatic way.

Just… structurally different.
Conversations feel scripted.
Reactions feel expected.
Everything feels like there’s a correct version of me I’m supposed to perform.
I can do it.

I just don’t understand why.
I understand systems.
I understand patterns.
I understand people.
But understanding something
and belonging inside it
are completely different things.
I don’t have many people I can call real.
Not because I don’t want to.

But because connection feels rare.
And forced connection feels worse than none.
“Averting your eyes isn’t running away.”
— Tsubasa Hanekawa
I don’t avoid things.

I just see too much of them.
Every interaction branches into possibilities.
Every word has weight.
Every silence says something.
It’s exhausting.
So I stay inside things that make sense.
Stories.
Systems.
Worlds that don’t pretend to be simple.
I write because it’s the only place where everything can exist at once.
“People don’t change. They reveal.”
— Deishuu Kaiki
Maybe that applies to me too.
I’m not becoming something.

I’m just slowly revealing what was already there.
And sometimes I wonder if that’s the problem.
“Not trying to be happy is cowardice.”
— Ougi Oshino
I don’t think that’s true.
I think trying to be something you’re not…
just to feel like you belong…
is worse.
So this is me.
Not fixed.
Not simplified.
Not explained.
Just… here.
